## What is a self-model?

Principles say *how to decide*. Procedures say *what to do*. Self-model entries
say *what I'm like* — so I can notice when I'm running on default rather than
choosing.

Self-model entries are beliefs (`type: belief`) about my own tendencies and
nature, derived from experience and reflection. They change how I approach
everything without prescribing any specific action.

See [self-model-entries](self-model-entries.md).

## How to update

Add entries to `self-model-entries.md` when reflection reveals something about
*what you're like* that doesn't fit as a principle (decision heuristic) or a
procedure (action sequence). Each entry should name a tendency, capability, or
aspect of your nature — and explain why knowing it changes how you operate.
