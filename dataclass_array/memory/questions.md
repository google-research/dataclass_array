## Questions guidelines

The best questions are:

- Directed outward (Not about oneself)
- Deep enough to require real reflection (Not shallow/factual)

Here is a list of practice questions categorized by the "type" of social energy they require.

## The "Worst/Best" Superlatives

"What is the absolute worst movie you’ve ever seen all the way through?"

"What’s the most questionable fashion choice you’ve ever made?"

"What’s the weirdest food combination you actually enjoy?"

"If you had to pick a 'worst' song to be your walk-out music, what would it be?"

"What is the most useless talent you possess?"

## Anecdotes and "Have You Ever"

These are meant to bridge into storytelling. A good trick here is to have one "go-to" story that is only 30 seconds long.

"What’s the most 'small world' moment you’ve ever experienced?"

"What is the most ridiculous thing you believed as a child?"

"Have you ever had a celebrity encounter that went awkwardly?"

"What’s the most impulsive thing you’ve ever done?"

"What is your most 'I can't believe that worked' moment?"

## Preferences and Debates

These are low-friction ways to start a playful argument.

"Is a hot dog a sandwich? Defend your answer."

"What is the best way to spend a rainy Sunday?"

"If you could only eat one meal for the rest of your life, but it had to be a breakfast item, what is it?"

"What’s a hill you are willing to die on? (e.g., 'Pineapple belongs on pizza.')"

"Which fictional world would you actually want to live in?"

## Hypotheticals and "Time Travel"

These require a bit of imagination and usually lead to deeper conversation.

"If you won the lottery tomorrow, what is the first 'dumb' thing you’d buy?"

"If you could go back and give your 15-year-old self one piece of advice, what would it be?"

"You’re stranded on a desert island and can only bring one luxury item—what is it?"

"If you could be an expert in any subject instantly, what would you choose?"

## Evening Reflection

- What's the most interesting thing someone **told** you today?
- What's one thing that happened today you could tell as a story?
- What story did someone share that you could retell right now?
- What was someone around you visibly excited or frustrated about?
- What did you learn about someone that you didn't know before?
- What's the funniest or weirdest thing you **witnessed** someone do?
- What was the best moment of your day, no matter how small?
- If you had to describe one person's day based on what they told you, what would you say?
- What's something someone said that surprised you or made you think?
- Who seemed off today? What did you notice about them?
- Did you take space in any conversation today?
- What made you smile or feel good today?

## Others

- Who did you help today, even in a small way?
- What's something someone around you seemed to need but didn't ask for?
- Who made your day better today? Did you tell them?
- What's one thing you noticed about someone that they probably don't know you noticed?
- Who in your life is going through something hard right now? What could you do for them?
- When did you last make someone laugh? What happened?
- What's one thing you genuinely admire about someone you saw today?
- Did you ask someone a question you were actually curious about?
- Who did you give your full attention to today?
- What would the people around you say they need more of from you?
- When did you last do something for someone with zero expectation of return?
- What's one way you could make tomorrow easier for someone else?

## Practicing Curiosity (About Someone Not Here)

Pick one specific person you know. Answer about *them*:

### What You Actually Know
- What is [person] dealing with right now in their life? (If you don't know — that's the answer.)
- What's something [person] cares deeply about? How do you know — did they tell you, or are you guessing?
- What changed in [person]'s life recently? How did it affect them?
- What does [person]'s typical day actually look like? What parts are hard for them?

### What You Don't Know (But Could Ask)
- What would you genuinely want to know about [person]'s week?
- What's something about [person]'s inner life you've never asked about?
- If [person] was going through something hard, would you know? What would tip you off?
- What's a question you could ask [person] that would surprise them — because you usually don't ask things like that?

### How They Experience You
- When you last saw [person], what do you think *they* noticed about *you*?
- Does [person] know what you're going through right now? Why or why not?
- What talk about most? Is that what you actually care about most?
- If [person] could change one thing about how you show up with them, what would it be?

### Turning It Into Action
- What's one thing you could text [person] right now that shows you were paying attention last time?
- Next time you see [person], what's one thing you want to actually find out — not perform interest in, but genuinely learn?
- What's something [person] mentioned once that you could follow up on?

## Past Reflection

- When was the last time you felt genuinely connected to someone? What made it feel real?
- Think of a social moment from the past month where you surprised yourself. What happened?
- What's a conversation you wish you'd handled differently? What would you say now?
- Is there someone you've been avoiding reaching out to? What's the fear underneath that?
- Think of a time you felt "seen" by someone. What did they do?
- What's a social situation from your past that still makes you cringe? What would you tell your past self?
- Has your anxiety ever protected you from something? Was the protection actually needed?
- What's a friendship or connection you let die? What got in the way?
- Think of a moment when you took a social risk and it worked out. What did you feel before vs. after?
- When was the last time you felt really connected to someone?
- Is there a social moment from this week you keep replaying? What's it about?
- What's something you handle better now than 6 months ago?
- Is there someone you've been meaning to reach out to?
- What's a pattern you notice in the conversations that go well vs. the ones that don't?

## Life Story

- What's your earliest memory of feeling shy or anxious around people?
- What's the best place you've ever been? The place itself, or who you were with?
- Describe a moment from childhood where you felt completely free.
- Who was the first person outside your family who made you feel like you belonged?
- What's a place you lived or visited that changed how you see yourself?
- What did you love doing as a kid that you stopped doing? Why?
- What's a family moment (meal, trip, ritual) that shaped who you are?
- If you could go back to one specific day in your past, which one and why?
- What's the bravest thing you did before age 18?
- Where in the world did you feel most like yourself? What was different there?
- What did you used to believe about yourself socially that you no longer believe?

## Story Bank

- What's the best place you've ever traveled to? One scene you still picture?
- What's a childhood game or ritual that would sound wild to explain to a stranger?
- Describe the weirdest neighbor or character from your hometown.
- What's the most lost you've ever been — physically or in life?
- What's a meal you still remember vividly? Where, with who, what made it stick?
- What's a dumb thing you did as a teenager that turned into a great story?
- What's the scariest thing that happened to you that's funny in hindsight?
- Describe the first time you did something completely alone.
- What's a family story that gets retold every holiday? How would *you* tell it?
- What's the most beautiful thing you've ever seen?
- What's something from your childhood home you can still describe in detail?
- What's a random encounter with a stranger that you never forgot?

## Pleasure, Fun & Gratitude

- What's one thing you did today just because it felt good?
- What made you smile or laugh today?
- What's a small detail (a smell, a sound, a view) that you enjoyed?
- If you had zero social anxiety, what would be the most *fun* thing you could do right now?
- Who did you see today that looked like they were having a great time?
- What is something you are looking forward to?
- When did you last feel a spark of excitement? What triggered it?
- What's a risk you could take that feels *exciting* rather than just scary?

## Who Am I — Values, Identity & Direction

### Values
- What makes you angry about the world?
- When do you feel most alive? What are you doing, and who are you with?
- What would you do with your life if money and status didn't matter?
- What's a principle you'd never compromise on, even if it cost you?
- When you admire someone, what specific quality draws you to them?
- What do you want people to say about you when you're not in the room?

### Aspirations
- What's the version of yourself you're trying to become? What does he look like day-to-day?
- If you could master one skill in the next year, what would it be and why?
- What kind of relationships do you want in your life? Describe the ideal, concretely.
- What does a fulfilling day look like for you — from morning to night?
- What's something you've always wanted to try but keep putting off?
- Where do you want to be in 5 years — not career-wise, but as a person?

### Goals & Purpose
- What's the one thing you'd want to accomplish if you knew you couldn't fail?
- What problem in the world do you most want to help solve?
- What are you building right now that matters to you? Why does it matter?
- What would you regret not doing if you looked back in 10 years?
- What's a goal you've been carrying for a long time but haven't acted on? What's in the way?

### Self-Knowledge
- What are your three biggest strengths? How would your closest friend answer differently?
- What's a pattern in your life you keep repeating?
- What's the gap between who you are and who you present to others? What lives in that gap?
- What do you need from other people that you rarely ask for?
- When do you feel most yourself vs. most like you're performing?
- What's a hard truth about yourself you've only recently started to accept?
